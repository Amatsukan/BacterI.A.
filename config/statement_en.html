The Goal

You control a bacterial colony on a Petri dish. Expand your territory, harvest nutrient spots for energy, and eliminate all enemy cells before the turn limit.
The player with the most cells and energy at the end wins.

 Rules

▶ The Map

  • A 64 × 64 square grid.
  • Player 0 starts with one cell at (0, 0) (top-left).
  • Player 1 starts with one cell at (63, 63) (bottom-right).
  • Nutrient spots are placed symmetrically (180° rotation).

▶ Nutrient Spots

Each spot has a type that determines its total energy reserve:

Type Code Total Energy
● Small 1 10
● Medium 2 30
● Large 3 70

If one of your cells occupies a nutrient spot, it automatically extracts 1 energy per turn until the spot is depleted.

▶ Energy

Starting energy is calculated from the distance between your base and the nearest nutrient spot, plus a safety margin. Energy is spent to perform actions.

▶ Actions (up to 5 per turn)

Action Syntax Cost Effect
EXPAND EXPAND x y 2 Place a new cell at (x, y). The target must be empty and adjacent (orthogonal or diagonal) to one of your existing cells. If the placement is invalid, the energy cost is still spent (wasted move).
ATTACK ATTACK x y 2 Attack an enemy cell at (x, y). Succeeds if you have strictly more allied neighbors around the target than the defender has. On success: the enemy cell is destroyed, your cell takes its place, and you gain +3 energy. On failure: energy is still spent.
AUTOPHAGY AUTOPHAGY x y 0 Sacrifice one of your own cells at (x, y). Recovers +1 energy.
WAIT WAIT 0 Do nothing.

Combine multiple actions with ; — Example: EXPAND 1 0; EXPAND 0 1; WAIT

The referee first collects both players lines, then resolves by global action phase: EXPAND → ATTACK → AUTOPHAGY → WAIT.

▶ Fog of War

  • You always see all nutrient spots on your half of the map.
  • Enemy cells and enemy-half spots are visible only within Chebyshev distance 3 of any of your cells.

▶ Victory Conditions

  1. Knockout — Destroy all of your opponent's cells.
  2. Turn limit (300 turns) — Highest score wins.
    Score = cells × 100 + energy
    Tiebreaker: total enemy cells destroyed during the game.

 Advanced Details

  • Each turn, all players receive input and output is collected before any action is resolved. Resolution order is fixed by phase: EXPAND, then ATTACK, then AUTOPHAGY, then WAIT.
  • If both players EXPAND into the same empty cell, the winner is the player with the highest adjacent allied-neighbor count around that target (from phase snapshot). If still tied, lower player index wins.
  • EXPAND adjacency and occupancy are validated on the phase snapshot; chain-expanding from newly created cells in the same turn is not possible.
  • ATTACK majority is computed from the attack-phase snapshot, then successful captures are applied deterministically by target coordinate order.
  • A failed EXPAND (occupied or not adjacent) still costs energy.
  • A failed ATTACK (no numerical majority) still costs energy.
  • A failed AUTOPHAGY (not your cell) costs nothing and gains nothing.
  • Passive nutrient extraction happens after all actions, once per turn.
  • The map has 180° rotational symmetry. Nutrient spots are guaranteed reachable from both starting positions.

 Game Protocol

Initialization input

Line 1: two space-separated integers:

  • mapSize — grid side length (always 64)
  • myIndex — your player index (0 or 1)

Line 2: one integer spotCount — number of nutrient spots visible on your half.

Next spotCount lines: three integers each:

  • x y type — position and type code (1=Small, 2=Medium, 3=Large)
Input per turn

Line 1: two integers myEnergy oppEnergy

Line 2: myCellCount

Next myCellCount lines: x y — coordinates of one of your cells

Next line: oppCellCount

Next oppCellCount lines: x y — coordinates of a visible enemy cell

Next line: visibleSpotCount

Next visibleSpotCount lines: x y type remainingEnergy — a visible nutrient spot

Output

A single line with up to 5 actions separated by ;

Example: EXPAND 1 0; ATTACK 3 4; WAIT

Execution order is deterministic and phase-based (see Advanced Details). Invalid EXPAND / ATTACK still consume energy when affordable.

You have 15 energy, opponent has 20. You see 1 own cell at (0,0), 0 enemy cells, and 1 nutrient spot (Small at 5,3 with 10 remaining). You spend 4 energy to place two new cells. Energy drops to 11.

Invalid action examples
  • EXPAND 10 10 on a non-adjacent cell: fails and still costs 2.
  • ATTACK 4 4 without numerical superiority: fails and still costs 2.
  • AUTOPHAGY 4 4 on a cell you do not own: fails, no gain, no cost.
